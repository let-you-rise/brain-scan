Warning Signs

Seek immediate medical attention if you experience any of these symptoms:

Chest Discomfort

Most heart attacks involve discomfort in the center of the chest that lasts more than a few minutes, or that goes away and comes back. It can feel like uncomfortable pressure, squeezing, fullness or pain.

Shortness of Breath

Often occurs with or before chest discomfort. It may be your only symptom. You may experience it while resting or during minimal physical activity.

Irregular Heartbeat

Heart palpitations or arrhythmias can feel like your heart is racing, pounding, fluttering, or skipping beats. Some arrhythmias can be life-threatening.

Cold Sweats

Unexplained sweating or clammy skin may be a sign of a heart attack or heart failure, especially when combined with other heart attack symptoms.

Fatigue

Unusual or extreme tiredness that may last for days, especially in women. You may feel exhausted with routine activities like walking or shopping.

Swelling

Swelling in the ankles, feet, legs, abdomen, or neck veins. This could be a sign that your heart isn't pumping blood effectively.

Risk Factors

Controllable Risk Factors
  • High blood pressure
  • High cholesterol
  • Smoking
  • Diabetes
  • Obesity
  • Unhealthy diet
  • Physical inactivity
  • Excessive alcohol use
  • Stress
Uncontrollable Risk Factors
  • Age (Risk increases with age)
  • Gender (Men have higher risk at younger age)
  • Family history of heart disease
  • Ethnicity (Higher risk in certain ethnic groups)
  • Previous heart attack or stroke

Note: Even if you have uncontrollable risk factors, managing controllable factors can significantly reduce your overall risk.

Prevention Strategies

Healthy Diet

Focus on heart-healthy foods:

  • Fruits and vegetables
  • Whole grains
  • Lean proteins
  • Low-fat dairy products
  • Healthy fats (olive oil, avocados, nuts)

Limit intake of:

  • Sodium (salt)
  • Saturated and trans fats
  • Added sugars
  • Processed foods
Physical Activity

Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity exercise per week:

  • Brisk walking
  • Swimming
  • Cycling
  • Dancing

Include strength training exercises 2-3 times per week.

Even small amounts of activity are better than none. Start slowly and build up gradually.

Regular Check-ups

Schedule regular health screenings to monitor:

  • Blood pressure
  • Cholesterol levels
  • Blood glucose
  • Body weight

Follow your doctor's recommendations for frequency of check-ups based on your personal risk factors.

Don't skip appointments, even if you feel fine.
